## LinkedIn (single post)

For a while I've had a renderer buried inside my imaging work that does one thing: it takes the pixels of a video and rebuilds them as a live field of points. Around 685,000 of them, redrawn every frame, in real time.

I finally gave it a name. PixelCluster.

Every region of every frame becomes a small cluster of points sharing that region's color. They spread, jitter, and drift on a turbulence field while the video plays through them. Move your cursor and the whole field responds.

You can try it in your browser right now. No install, no account. Drop in any clip and watch it resolve into points. If you want to keep it, the app is free for Mac and Windows.

ciamac.com/pixelcluster
