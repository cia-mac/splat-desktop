Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 24, 2026

Gsplat does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. Your videos stay on your device. There are no accounts, no analytics, and no tracking.

What Gsplat Does

Gsplat is a desktop application for macOS and Windows that transforms video files into real-time particle visualizations. You select a local video file, and the app renders it as a cloud of glowing particles. You can also record the output as a video file saved to your machine.

Data Collection

None. Gsplat does not collect any personal information. There are no user accounts, no sign-ups, no cookies, no analytics, and no telemetry.

Your Video Files

All video processing happens locally on your device. Your files are never uploaded, transmitted, or shared with any server. Recorded output files are saved to a location you choose on your machine.

Network Requests

Gsplat makes one external network request: it loads the Three.js library from a public CDN (cdn.jsdelivr.net) when the app launches. This is a widely used open-source JavaScript library required for 3D rendering. No user data is sent in this request.

Beyond that, the app makes no network calls. It does not phone home, check for updates automatically, or communicate with any backend service.

Third-Party Services

Gsplat does not integrate with any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking services. The only third-party involvement is the CDN-hosted Three.js library mentioned above. jsDelivr's own privacy policy governs their CDN infrastructure.

Data Storage

Gsplat may store application preferences (such as window size or last-used settings) locally on your device using standard OS mechanisms. This data never leaves your machine.

Children's Privacy

Gsplat does not collect data from anyone, including children. There is nothing to comply with because there is nothing collected.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted here with a new effective date. Given that the app collects no data, material changes are unlikely.
